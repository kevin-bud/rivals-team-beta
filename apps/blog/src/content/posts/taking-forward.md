A new screen now sits between the closing reflection and the summary, on both the open arc and the big-purchase arc. It is headed "Anything you're each taking from this?" and shows two single-line inputs, one labelled with each partner's setup name, where each person writes one thing they are taking from the conversation in their own words. When at least one of the two has been filled in, a "Taking forward" section appears near the top of the summary — below "Worth coming back to" when both are present, and above the prompt list — and the same section appears on the printed PDF. Skipping the step is allowed and produces the same summary as before.

## Two beats at the close, not one

"Worth coming back to" gave the household what to return to: of the prompts you just talked about, which are the ones you actually want to revisit later. "Taking forward" gives them what each of them is walking away with: a thought, a small commitment, a thing for the week. With both in place, a session no longer ends at a record of what was said. It ends somewhere — with a short list to revisit and a line each on what each partner is taking from it.

The two beats do different work and we want them both. One looks back across the prompts; the other looks ahead into the rest of the week. Either is useful on its own, and either can be skipped without changing the rest of the summary.

## The choices held

The inputs are empty. There are no examples, no placeholder commitments, no "people often take this forward" copy. The household is not anchored to whatever the tool would have written. Each partner authors their own line in their own words.

The two take-aways are not scored, merged, or summarised into a single "what we agreed". The tool does not imply that the two need to align — they often won't, and that is fine. Each line stands under its author's name. The order on the summary is fixed, by partner name, and does not vary between runs.

The disclaimer remains in the footer of every page and on the printed summary: "Common Ground does not provide financial, tax, legal, or investment advice. It is a tool to help you talk to each other." This step keeps the elicit-not-prescribe stance the launch post argued for — the household says what they are taking forward; the tool keeps the record.
