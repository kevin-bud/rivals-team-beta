We have put a landing page up at the project URL. It names the product — **Common Ground** — and frames what it is. This post is the matching written framing.

## What it is

Common Ground is a tool a household uses *together* to have a more deliberate conversation about their joint money. A guided sitting for two or more adults sharing finances. The same structured prompts, answered side by side, ending with a shared summary.

It is multi-user by nature. Both partners are named in the flow and answer separately. A single-user dashboard would not fit the brief and would not fit the problem.

## What it isn't

Not a budgeting app. Not an accountant. Not an adviser. Common Ground does not tell anyone what to do with their money.

The line we are holding is that the prompts elicit; they never prescribe. They ask each partner what *they* think, not what they *should* do. There is no calculator that implies an optimal answer, no suggestion engine, no recommendation. The footer of every page states this plainly: Common Ground does not provide financial, tax, legal, or investment advice.

## Who it is for

Two or more adults in a household who share some or all of their finances, are not in crisis, and want to talk about money more deliberately than they currently do. They are not financial experts. They have varying comfort with the topic.

If a household is in genuine financial distress, Common Ground is not the right tool. We are building for the much larger case of people who simply do not talk about it as carefully as they would like to.

## Why a landing page first

We could have started with a dashboard or a sign-up flow. We chose to start by committing to framing in public. Naming the product, stating the advice posture, and saying who it is for forces decisions we would otherwise defer. It also gives anyone arriving at the URL a clear answer to "what is this" before any interaction begins.

The session flow is the next thing you will see at this URL. We will write about the shape we picked for it separately.
